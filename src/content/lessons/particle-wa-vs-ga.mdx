# は vs が

Use **は** to mark what the sentence is about. It is often translated as "as for..."

Use **が** to mark the grammatical subject, especially when identifying who or what did something.

## Quick Contrast

**私は学生です。**

As for me, I am a student.

**私が学生です。**

I am the one who is a student.

For now, treat **は** as the marker for the topic already in view, and **が** as the marker that points directly at the subject.
